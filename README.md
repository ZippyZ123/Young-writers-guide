<h1>Young Writer's Guide</h1>

<h2>Writing Tips</h2>

<p>Tip no.1: Use words like benevolent or malicious instead of good or bad. Trust me, it makes your work way better.</p>

<p>Tip no.2: Don't say "sandwich". Say "peculiar, putrid, soggy sandwich". The reader instantly knows what the sandwich looks like (and anything else you'll describe).</p>

<p>Tip no.3: Use powerful verbs. Instead of "walked", say stomped, tip‑toed, slithered, lurched, or marched.</p>

<p>Tip no.4: Add exaggeration. Say "a mountain of homework" instead of "a lot of homework". It makes your writing fun and dramatic.</p>

<p>Tip no.5: Use sensory details. Describe how things smell, sound, feel, taste, and look. It pulls the reader into your world.</p>

<p>Tip no.6: Make characters dramatic. Give them quirks: a boy who only whispers, a girl who collects broken pencils, a teacher who faints at long words.</p>

<p>Tip no.7: Use unexpected comparisons. "Her scream was like a fire alarm eating a trumpet."</p>

<p>Tip no.8: Short sentences for drama. When something BIG happens, short sentences hit harder.</p>

<p>Tip no.9: Dialogue shows personality. Don’t just write what they say — show how they say it.</p>

<p>Tip no.10: End with a punch. The last line should feel like a door slamming or a firework popping.</p>

<h2>Young Writer's Thesaurus</h2>

<p><strong>Instead of "good"</strong>: benevolent, admirable, splendid, noble, gracious.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "bad"</strong>: malicious, vile, dreadful, wicked, corrupt.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "walk"</strong>: stomp, tiptoe, slither, shuffle, march.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "said"</strong>: whispered, bellowed, muttered, snapped, announced.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "big"</strong>: colossal, gigantic, enormous, towering, massive.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "small"</strong>: minuscule, tiny, microscopic, petite, slight.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "angry"</strong>: furious, enraged, livid, fuming, seething.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "happy"</strong>: jubilant, delighted, ecstatic, gleeful, overjoyed.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "scary"</strong>: terrifying, petrifying, sinister, chilling, haunting.</p>

<p><strong>Instead of "boring"</strong>: dull, tedious, monotonous, lifeless, stale.</p>
